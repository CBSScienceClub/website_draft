About CEBS

The Centre for Excellence in Basic Sciences (CEBS) is a joint academic venture of the University of Mumbai (UM) and the Department of Atomic Energy (DAE), Government of India. The institute primarily offers 5-year integrated MSc courses in four streams of basic sciences, namely Physics, Chemistry, Biology and Mathematics.
Students join the centre through the National Entrance Screening Test (NEST) after completing 10+2 or equivalent level of schooling. All four subjects are taught in the first year, and students can opt the stream of their choice second year onwards.

About the Club

We are a team of student volunteers from different years and streams, and take an active part in building a safe and inquisitive environment at the institute. Through our sessions we also wish to motivate independent pursuit of knowledge, and strengthen the relationship of the community with the world of research. Read more in our blog.

Rendezvous

In these sessions we invite researchers and host a lively dialogue of students with them. The conversation often begins around a central scientific theme in an informal and comfortable setting. We facilitate and encourage students to ask questions, which bring out more elaborate perspectives from the discussion.

Open mic

Open Mics provide students with an opportunity to explore their scientific interests and share them with the community. The session begins with 5-6 students briefly introducing a topic of their interest. This is followed by a discussion session, where the speakers elaborate on their topic with smaller groups of students in greater depth.

Interactive session

Here we invite researchers and have free-flowing conversations with them on a variety of topics. For example, we try to understand the problems certain sections of our society are facing and how we can try to solve them. We also talk about their impressions and experiences throughout their journey in academia.

Baithak

Baithak is a unique platform for students to express their thoughts and feelings. It provides us with an opportunity to understand fellow students at a more personal level. The format is very similar to open mics, except that what students want to share need not be prepared beforehand.

Counselling

We have had a variety of such events in the past, where we invite some of our professors to advise us all as to how we can make the best utilization of our academic opportunities. We talk about how we can build our interest in science and research, the academic life ahead of our current program, and so on.
